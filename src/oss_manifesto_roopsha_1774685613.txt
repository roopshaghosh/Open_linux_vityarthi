For me, open source is not just about software — it is about people.
It is about strangers helping each other grow without expecting
anything in return.

As a student, I use tools like Git almost every day. Behind these
tools are developers who decided to share their work openly, making
it easier for learners like me to understand, practice, and build.
This idea of sharing knowledge freely is what inspires me the most.

Open source creates a space where learning never stops. Even small
contributions, like fixing a bug or improving documentation, can
make a difference. It shows that anyone can be a part of something
bigger, no matter their level of experience.

Right now, I am still learning and exploring. I may not contribute
much yet, but I am slowly building the confidence to do so. In the
future, I want to create my own projects and make them open so that
others can learn from them, just like I am learning today.

This is not just a goal, but a responsibility I want to carry
forward.
